Still deciding

Good questions. Honest answers.

I'm completely new to AI. Am I going to get left behind? +
No — the non-technical track assumes zero prior knowledge, and the first two weeks are shared across both tracks to build a common foundation. The Week Zero orientation and the Stuck Protocol are specifically designed for people who've been burned by "beginner-friendly" courses that weren't.
I'm already a developer. Is this too basic for me? +
The technical track assumes Python literacy but no ML background. If you've already deployed production RAG systems and fine-tuned models, you'll find Month 1 light — but Month 2 and 3 cover modern AI engineering (agents, MCP, evals, production LLM patterns) at depth. We can send you the full syllabus to judge.
Can I switch tracks mid-program? +
Yes, until the end of Week 4. After that the tracks have diverged too much. The Week Zero placement call is designed to get this right the first time.
How many hours per week should I expect? +
Around 8 hours per week — roughly 3 hours of lessons, 1.5 hours of live session, and 3–4 hours on the weekly deliverable. It's designed to be done alongside a full-time job.
What if I fall behind? +
Week 8 is a built-in "flex week" with lighter deliverables and catch-up time. If you miss two consecutive weeks, the instructor personally reaches out to help you plan a path back. All materials are also yours for life, so you can continue after the cohort ends.
Who actually teaches this, and who reviews the capstone? +
The lead instructor is supported by a small team of mentors, all working AI practitioners. Capstones are reviewed by a rotating panel of 2–3 industry professionals drawn from our network. Instructor and mentor bios are on the About page.
Is the certificate actually recognized? +
CAIE is an independently issued credential — not accredited by a university body. What it is: verifiable (via public URL), rigorous (requires deliverables, capstone, and exam to earn), and defensible (your portfolio is evidence). Most employers in AI care more about demonstrated skill than formal accreditation — which is why we focus on what you built, not just what you passed.
Refund policy? +
Full refund if you withdraw before the end of Week 2 — no questions asked. After that, the cohort is committed. If you don't pass the certification exam, you get one free retake within 90 days of program end.
